The philosophy

Why Drag a Toddler Through TSA?

Because kids are adults in training.

Travel teaches them the world is bigger than their backyard. They learn to navigate new places, try new foods, hear new languages, and adapt when things don't go as planned.

"But they won't remember it!" — Maybe not. But you will. And those experiences shape who they become, whether they remember the details or not.

The best time to start traveling with your kids isn't "when they're older." It's now.

FAQ

Common Questions

Is this just for rich people?

Nope! We're middle class. Points level the playing field — you earn them on everyday purchases.

Do I need to spend a lot?

Not at all. Gas, groceries, diapers — stuff you're already buying adds up fast with the right cards.

What about annual fees?

Some cards have fees ($95-695), but benefits usually outweigh them. We break down the math.

Is this legal?

Absolutely! Credit card companies want you to use their cards. They make money from merchants.

How long until my first trip?

Most families earn enough for domestic in 3-6 months, international in 6-12 months.

Is it complicated?

It can be. That's why we break it down into simple, actionable steps. No spreadsheets required.
